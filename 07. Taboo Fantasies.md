## What Makes Something Taboo?

Taboo isn't about harm—it's about social rules. Things we're taught not to want, not to think about, not to admit.

Age differences. Power dynamics. Family roleplay. Religious scenarios. These are taboo not because they're inherently wrong, but because they break social conventions.

The key distinction: fantasy versus reality. In fantasy, we explore the forbidden safely. We break rules without consequences. We experience the thrill of transgression without the harm.
